main genres of cinema

action

Action film is a genre wherein physical action takes precedence in the storytelling. The film will often have continuous motion and action including physical stunts, chases, fights, battles, and races. The story usually revolves around a hero that has a goal, but is facing incredible odds to obtain it. Every action movie seems to involve a hero facing countless obstacles, getting into numerous fights, and being portrayed by an actor well into his 60’s who really should have moved on to prestige films or maybe plays by now. But in truth there are many different types of action movie genres, all with their own defining features, dialogue, and budget fiascos.

1. Action Adventure

Typically, these films take place in exotic locations and frequently involve the hero travelling to several locations around the world. There are a lot of films with this combined genre. Examples include the Indiana Jones franchise, and most superhero films are action adventure, too.

2. Action Comedy

The action comedy subgenre involves action and humour. The subgenre became a popular trend in the 1980s, when actors who were known for their background in comedy, such as Eddie Murphy, began to take roles in action films.

3. Action Drama

Action drama is a subgenre involving action and drama.

Examples include Man on Fire (2004), Top Gun (1986), Lingaa (2014) and Jai Simha (2016).

4. Action Horror

A subgenre combining the intrusion of an evil force, event, or supernatural personage of horror movies with the gunfights and frenetic chases of the action genre. Themes or elements often prevalent in typical action-horror films include gore, demons, vicious animals, vampires and, most commonly, zombies. This category can also take elements from the fantasy genre.

5. Sci-fi Action

Sharing many of the conventions of a science fiction film, sci-fi action films emphasizes gun-play, space battles, invented weaponry, and other sci-fi elements weaved into action film premises.

Examples include G.I. Samurai (1979), The Matrix (1999), the Star Wars franchise, the Men in Black franchise, Aliens (1986), Transformers (2007), The Hunger Games (2012), Equilibrium (2002), Akira (1988), RoboCop (1987), Avatar (2009), They Live (1988), and The Fifth Element (1997).

6. Action Thriller

Featuring guns, cool explosions, and amazing set pieces, this movie subgenre first developed in the 1970s in such films as Dirty Harry (1971) and The French Connection (1971), and became the exemplar of the Hollywood mega-blockbuster in the 1980s in such works as Die Hard (1988) and Lethal Weapon (1987). These films often feature a race against the clock, lots of violence, and a clear—often flamboyantly evil—antagonist. Though they may involve elements of crime or mystery films, those aspects take a back seat to the action.

Othersignificant works include Hard Boiled (1992), True Romance (1993), Point Break (2015), The Warriors (1979), Bullitt (1968), Cobra (1986), Rambo: First Blood Part II (1985), and Taken (2008).
